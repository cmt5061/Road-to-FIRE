📐 How the SWR is calculated
Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR) is now derived from your actual retirement horizon: Life Expectancy − Target Retirement Age. This is more precise than age brackets — a 40-year-old retiring at 65 needs a shorter horizon SWR than one retiring at 40, even though they're the same age today.
The SWR table follows the 2026 ERN/Bengen research consensus: ≤20 years → 4.5% · 21–25 yrs → 4.0% · 26–30 yrs → 3.75% · 31–40 yrs → 3.5% · 40+ yrs → 3.25%
Target Retirement Age also feeds the Coast FIRE calculation — reverse-discounting your FIRE number so growth alone gets you there without further contributions.
